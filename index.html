I. Illustration of the Generation Process

This sample corresponds to the example treated in Section 4.1 of the paper. It showcases the effect of changing the instrument name in the prompt at different timesteps of a 50-step inference process. As discussed in the paper, if we swap the instrument name in the prompt during the early stages of the sampling process, the content is not fully preserved. On the other hand, if we do so during the later stages of inference, the timbre information has already been integrated into the latent, making it too late to modify at this point. By selecting an appropriate timestep, the desired outcome is achieved: playing the original content with the target instrument.

III. Comparison with MusicMagus

In this section, we compare our method, Diff-TONE, with MusicMagus [1]. It is important to note that the original audio inputs differ between the two approaches: MusicMagus is built upon AudioLDM 2 [2], while our method is based on Stable Audio Open [3]. Additionally, for MusicMagus, we retained the original hyperparameter settings, including 100 diffusion steps, whereas our approach uses only 50 diffusion steps.

V. Limitations

Sometimes, our approach fails when a non-optimal timestep is selected. This can result in either generating different content than intended, or, producing audio that is identical to the original input.
